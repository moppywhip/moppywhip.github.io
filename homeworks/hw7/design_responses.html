    The Gestalt principles are a group of visual perception principles developed by German psychologists in the 1920s. They are based on the idea that "an organized whole is perceived as greater than the sum of its parts." The principles are used in UI design to help create effective visual communication and improve user experience. For example, the proximity principle can be used in UI design to group similar information and organize content, and the common region principle can be used to create visual hierarchies and guide the user's attention.
